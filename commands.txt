# commands for viewing jobs on hydra

1. Monitoring Your Specific Job

Use these to see exactly what is happening with your request:

    squeue -j <job_id> --start: Shows the estimated start time (as seen in your snippet).

    scontrol show job <job_id>: Provides a deep dive into the job, including the full command run, memory requested, and detailed reasons for any delays.

    sprio -j <job_id>: Shows your priority score components (Age, Fairshare, JobSize).

2. Monitoring the Queue and Others

To see where you stand relative to the rest of the cluster:

    squeue -u $USER: The standard check for your own running and pending jobs.

    mysqueue: A VSC-specific shortcut that often provides a cleaner, summarized view of your jobs.

    squeue -p pascal_gpu: Shows everyone waiting or running in the pascal_gpu partition so you can see how many people are ahead of you.

    squeue -t R: Shows only Running jobs across the whole cluster.

    squeue -t PD: Shows only Pending jobs across the whole cluster.

3. Cluster and Resource Info

Use these to find "empty" spots or check node health:

    sinfo: Displays a general overview of all partitions and their states (idle, mix, alloc, down).

    sinfo -p pascal_gpu -N: Shows the status of every individual node in the Pascal GPU partition.

    pestat -u $USER: A very helpful utility to see the CPU and memory usage of your running jobs in real-time.

    sacctmgr show user $USER: Shows your account associations and limits.

4. Historical Data

If a job finishes and you want to know how it performed:

    sacct -j <job_id>: Shows the final state (Completed, Failed, OutOfMemory) and the elapsed time.

    sacct -j <job_id> --format=JobID,JobName,MaxRSS,Elapsed: Specifically checks the maximum memory (MaxRSS) used, which is vital for debugging "Out of Memory" crashes.

5. Management Commands

    scancel <job_id>: Kills the job immediately.

    scontrol hold <job_id>: Pauses your job in the queue without deleting it.

    scontrol release <job_id>: Resumes a held job.
